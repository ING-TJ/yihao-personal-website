# Content Guide

## Editorial principles

Write with evidence, specificity and restraint. The site should sound like an international young scholar and environmental entrepreneur—not a corporate advertisement, conventional CV or inflated personal brand.

## Verification levels

1. **Confirmed:** supported by the owner, an official certificate, publication record or public institutional page.
2. **Draft:** wording is prepared but awaits Yihao’s approval.
3. **Private:** supplied for internal use and not published.
4. **Unknown:** keep the field empty; the interface hides it or presents an honest empty state.

Never convert “unknown” into plausible-sounding copy.

## Research entries

Each research direction should include a clear question, a 100–180 Chinese-character overview, one authorized image, related verified outputs and an optional detail link. Experimental results, target compounds, locations and collaborator names require source confirmation.

## Publications

Use the typed structure in `src/data/publications.ts`. DOI values must be canonical and resolvable. Mark manuscripts accurately as Published, Under Review or In Preparation; do not imply acceptance.

## Images

Use WebP or AVIF for web delivery. Keep the longest image edge appropriate to its display size and remove sensitive metadata. Every image needs permission, meaningful alternative text and a short source note in internal records.

## Translation workflow

English and Simplified Chinese are the editorial bases. Update both first, then translate the same meaning—not individual words—into the remaining languages. Complete the checks in `TRANSLATION_REVIEW_REQUIRED.md` before publication.
